·Why Clinics Buy This

A specific, painful problem aesthetic clinics and dental clinics in Egypt and the Gulf (and later the US) already pay people to solve badly. These two verticals get the most DMs, charge premium prices, and lose the most money to no-shows — so they pay the most for a fix. We solve it 24/7 for less than the cost of a part-time receptionist.

The pain

  • Leads are lost — when a patient doesn't book on first contact, no automated follow-up is sent back · they drift away
  • No-shows pile up — no automated confirmation before the appointment so patients forget and don't show up
  • No post-visit follow-up — care instructions, Google review requests, return appointments all skipped
  • Social channels scattered — Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, Messenger, comments — each in its own app, nothing centralized
  • No patient tagging — can't easily go back to past leads or patients with targeted offers
  • No data sync — nothing flows automatically to a Google Sheet or CRM, no categorization, manual entry every time
  • Volume overwhelm — 100–500 DMs/week, 80% the same 5 questions (price, hours, slots, location, photos)
  • Receptionists burn out — most of the day re-typing the same answers · after-hours and weekend inquiries go cold

What we replace

  • Hiring a 2nd receptionist (~$800–1,500/mo in Egypt + the Gulf)
  • Cobbled-together Manychat / Zapier / Calendly stacks
  • Generic chatbots that hallucinate or sound robotic
  • Lost ad spend on leads that never get a reply
  • Missed appointment bookings at night & on weekends

What changes for the clinic (each pain → V1 fix)

  • Lost leads come back — clinic builds its own Custom Follow-Up Rules (no-code rule builder): trigger ("didn't book in 24h") → reply → delay. Plus every inquiry auto-tagged in the built-in pipeline.
  • No-shows drop — automatic 24h + 2h reminders; if a patient wants to reschedule, human handoff routes to staff (multi-step rebook flow is V2)
  • One inbox for social DMs — WhatsApp on every plan; Instagram + Messenger unlock on Growth and above as a single all-in-one dashboard (Telegram · SMS · web chat · Google Business come in V2)
  • Every patient tagged — auto-tagged by source, service, urgency, status · custom tags too · all tags live in our native pipeline (visual filter / retargeting dashboard is V2)
  • Auto data captured — every inquiry & booking lands in our built-in lead pipeline with name · phone · channel · source · service · tags · status · appointment time (external CRM / Google Sheets sync is V2)
  • Bookings happen 24/7 — bot pulls free slots from our built-in calendar, patient picks, appointment is confirmed, owner gets an instant email notification
  • Owner stays in control — bot only sends the pre-approved replies the clinic wrote in the Clinic Setup Wizard · never invents text · no hallucinations
  • Optional clinic website — every plan includes ready-made aesthetic / dental website templates (1 on Starter · up to 5 on Agency · unlimited white-label on Enterprise) — pulls all content from the Setup Wizard, no separate site to maintain
  • Basic analytics — 6 simple counters: messages answered · bookings made · conversion rate · top-requested services · channel performance · handoff cases

Multi-step rebook · post-visit care · satisfaction surveys · advanced analytics dashboard · external CRM/Sheets sync · voice/phone AI → V2.

·The Patient Journey — Three Phases, Fully Automated

From inquiry to satisfied patient, the bot handles every step. Three phases ladder from seconds → minutes → days/weeks.

A Inquiry → Reply Seconds
  1. Patient sends a DM — WhatsApp on every plan; Instagram + Messenger on Growth and above (V1 channels). Any source (ad click, story tag, website CTA, organic post, profile).
  2. Bot sends the welcome message the clinic wrote in the Setup Wizard — same on every channel, every source
  3. Intent matching classifies the message: price · services · location · hours · booking · before/after photos · human help
  4. Sends the matching fixed reply from the clinic's Fixed-Reply FAQ Library — predictable, on-brand, EN or AR auto-detected
  5. Patient auto-tagged in the built-in pipeline — by lead source, service interest, and status
B Booking Minutes
  1. Patient signals booking intent ("I want an appointment" · "Available tomorrow?" · CTA click)
  2. Bot pulls free slots live from our built-in booking calendar — only inside working hours, no double-booking
  3. Patient picks a slot from the carousel / quick-reply buttons
  4. Bot confirms the booking + logs it in the built-in lead pipeline + sends the owner an instant email notification
C Lifecycle Days → weeks
  1. Reminders auto-sent 24h + 2h before the appointment to cut no-shows
  2. Custom Follow-Up Rules kick in for unconverted leads — clinic-built triggers ("asked about price, didn't book in 24h") send the clinic's pre-written follow-up reply automatically
  3. Reschedule / cancel — patient asks → human handoff routes to staff (auto-reschedule via bot is V2)
  4. Booking status logged in the built-in pipeline — clinic owns the data and can run manual follow-ups against any tag
  5. V2 (later): 48h reminder · auto no-show recovery · post-visit care + medication · satisfaction surveys · return-appointment suggestion

The bot never writes its own messages. It just classifies, picks from the clinic's reply list, and sends. The only "live" data it inserts is the doctor's free time slots, reminder timestamps, and survey results.

These 14 features are the entire V1 scope. Anything else is V2 — no exceptions.

  • 6 · Built-in Booking Calendar · our own native calendar — patients book slots directly, staff can edit appointments in-app. No Google Calendar or Outlook required.
  • 7 · Email Notification on Booking · clinic owner / receptionist gets an instant email when a new appointment is booked.
  • 8 · Appointment Reminders · automatic reminders 24h and 2h before the appointment to reduce no-shows.
  • 9 · Human Handoff · if the bot is unsure or the patient asks for a person, staff can take over from the dashboard.
  • 10 · Built-in Lead Pipeline · our own native pipeline view — every inquiry and booking logged with name · phone · channel · source · service · tags · status · appointment time. No external CRM or spreadsheet required.
  • 11 · Basic Analytics · simple counters: messages answered · bookings made · booking conversion rate · top-requested services · channel performance · handoff cases.

04Long-Term Acquisition Strategy

Phase 1 (§03) is the standing start — founders + warm network + a single $100 influencer post. From Phase 2 (Jul 2027) onward, acquisition runs on five repeatable engines split across outbound, inbound, events, word of mouth, and discovery. Each engine has an owner, a tool stack, and a unit economic we steer toward as we scale to ~230 clinics by end-2027 and ~660 by end-2028.

1 · Outbound — sales team led

Owner: sales reps + sales manager (post-Phase-2 hires). The high-volume engine. Each rep targets 10 new clinics / month across four outbound surfaces in parallel:

  • Cold calls — public clinic phone numbers (Google Maps, medical directories, Yelp-equivalent in MENA). Reps run 30–50 calls/day in Arabic / English.
  • LinkedIn outreach — DMs to clinic owners, medical directors, aesthetic-practice managers. Two-step: connect → value DM → book demo.
  • WhatsApp outreach — scraped public clinic numbers + a 30s Loom in Arabic. Highest open rate in MENA (~95%).
  • Cold email — bilingual EN / AR drip (3 emails over 10 days) with demo Loom + 14-day free-trial invite.

Tool stack — subscribe first, build later. While team is small (≤4 reps) we subscribe to Apollo / Lemlist / Instantly / La Growth Machine for LinkedIn auto-outreach + email sequencing. Once we cross ~$30k MRR we build our own lightweight outreach SaaS in-house (LinkedIn auto-connect, email cadences, WhatsApp blast queue) — same engine our clinics use, dog-fooded for our sales team. Saves ~$300–500/mo per seat and gives us a second product line down the road.

2 · Inbound — website + paid + organic + partners

Owner: marketer + part-time content writer (post-Phase-2 hires). Inbound = anything that brings a clinic to our website. Target: ~25% of total acquisition by end of 2027.

  • Paid ads — Meta (Instagram + Facebook) + Google Search + LinkedIn for clinic-owner ICP. Start at $500/mo budget H2 2027, scale by CAC.
  • SEO — bilingual blog 2–4 posts/week targeting "AI receptionist for clinics", "WhatsApp booking aesthetic clinic Dubai/Riyadh", "بوت رد على عيادات", competitor-comparison pages, programmatic city pages.
  • Affiliates & partners (fixed fee or revshare) — clinic consultants, dental supply distributors, aesthetic equipment resellers, marketing agencies serving clinics. Pay per closed deal or a fixed monthly retainer. Nanno (clinic services agency) is the first partner — co-marketed offering where Nanno's clinic clients get a discounted AI receptionist alongside Nanno's services.
  • Reddit + niche forums — answer questions on r/dentistry, r/medspa, r/Egypt, r/dubai, MENA clinic-owner Telegram groups. Soft mentions only.
  • Organic social — LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok 3–5×/week. Build-in-public, case studies, "before/after AI" reels from real clinics.
  • Doctor-community newsletters — sponsor or guest-write in aesthetic-medicine + dental newsletters across MENA + (later) US.

3 · Events · webinars · doctor connections

Owner: Mahmoud + marketer. High-touch, high-trust — best ROI per clinic-owner hour spent.

  • Industry events & expos — AEEDC Dubai (dental), Dubai Derma, IDS (when we expand), Cairo Aesthetic Congress. Booth or speaker slot from H1 2028 onward.
  • Monthly webinars — "How Cairo clinics doubled booking rate with AI", "WhatsApp playbook for aesthetic clinics". Free, 45 min, ends with demo + 14-day trial.
  • Doctor-network connections — Belal + Mahmoud + Ahmed each leverage their personal medical / clinic-owner networks for warm intros. Roll into a formal medical advisory board by H2 2027 (2–3 KOL doctors per region).
  • Local meetups — small in-person dinners with 6–10 clinic owners in Cairo / Dubai / Riyadh once we have a city manager.

4 · Word of mouth — between doctors

Owner: customer success + every founder. Doctors talk to doctors. By end-2027 we want word-of-mouth contributing ~15–20% of new clinics.

  • Referral program (always-on) — both sides get 1 free month for every successful clinic referral. Stacks unlimited.
  • Case studies — 1 published case study per region per quarter. "Dubai clinic booked X appointments in 30 days" with named doctor + numbers.
  • Influencer-doctor partnerships — repeat the $100 Phase 1 post motion 2–4×/quarter with different ICP-matched doctors per region.
  • Customer-advisory group — top 20 clinics get a quarterly Zoom, early-access features, and a small annual gift. They become organic evangelists.
  • NPS & review surfaces — chase Trustpilot / G2 / Capterra reviews from happy clinics. Reviews compound for years.

5 · More ways to discover — emerging surfaces we'll test

Owner: marketer + founders. Each quarter we pick one experimental surface and run a 6–8 week test with a small budget ceiling. Anything that produces a clinic at < $300 CAC graduates into an engine.

  • Google My Business + Maps presence — partner with clinic-marketing tools so clinics discover us when they optimize their listing.
  • YouTube — long-form "how to run a clinic in 2027" videos in Arabic + English. Long tail, compounds for years.
  • TikTok organic — short clinic-owner tips in Arabic / Gulf dialects. Cheap experiment.
  • Affiliate marketplaces — list on AppSumo / Saaszilla / regional SaaS-deal platforms for spike sign-ups.
  • Co-marketing with adjacent SaaS — pair with CRM tools, calendar tools, payment processors (Tap, Paymob) for joint webinars + shared lead lists.
  • Regional press — Forbes Middle East, Wamda, MENAbytes, Magnitt — once we have a fundable story.
  • Conferences as speakers — when team can spare a founder day, speaking slots beat booths for credibility.
  • Open API + integrations marketplace — once Zapier / Make / Pipedream are live (V2), our integrations directory becomes a discovery channel of its own.
How the engines mix as we scale:
  • Phase 1 (Aug–Dec 2026): 100% founders + warm-network + 1 influencer post. No paid sales team yet.
  • H1 2027: founders still drive; first inbound flywheel turns (SEO + organic social compound).
  • H2 2027 (Phase 2 funded): outbound becomes the dominant engine — 3 sales reps + 1 marketer ship ~37 gross adds/mo. Inbound ramps to ~25%.
  • 2028: outbound still leads (~50% of new clinics), inbound ~25%, word-of-mouth ~15–20%, events / partners / experiments fill the rest.

Top 3 risks

1 — Channel API approval timelines. Meta WhatsApp Business + Instagram Graph + Messenger Platform API onboarding can take 2–4 weeks each (sometimes longer for new businesses). Mitigation: apply for Meta Business + WhatsApp Business in May, not July. Messenger and Instagram run on the same Meta Business approval. Have manual handoff ready as a fallback if any channel is still pending approval at launch.

2 — Clinic-owner perception + receptionist pushback. Owners may initially assume it's a typical chatbot that "talks" to patients (and worry it could say the wrong thing), or staff worry they're being replaced. Mitigation: the bot can't go off-script — it only sends replies the clinic typed itself. Demo it by showing the reply-list editor and saying "the bot can't send anything that isn't already in this list, written by you." For staff: frame it as "kills the boring 80% of repeat DMs so you focus on the patients who actually book." Every clinic gets a 60-min onboarding to write the initial reply library together.

3 — Patient data privacy (GDPR · UAE PDPL · KSA PDPL). Patient messages contain personal + sometimes health-adjacent info. Mitigation: data residency in EU or UAE where required, end-to-end encryption at rest, no sharing across clinics, clear DPA in the ToS. Avoid US HIPAA territory in Phase 1 by sticking to non-medical aesthetic clinics — we revisit for medical/dental in Phase 2.
