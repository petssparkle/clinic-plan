·Why Clinics Buy This

A specific, painful problem aesthetic clinics and dental clinics in Egypt and the Gulf (and later the US) already pay people to solve badly. These two verticals get the most DMs, charge premium prices, and lose the most money to no-shows — so they pay the most for a fix. We solve it 24/7 for less than the cost of a part-time receptionist.

The pain

  • Leads are lost — when a patient doesn't book on first contact, no automated follow-up is sent back · they drift away
  • No-shows pile up — no automated confirmation before the appointment so patients forget and don't show up
  • No post-visit follow-up — care instructions, Google review requests, return appointments all skipped
  • Social channels scattered — Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, Messenger, comments — each in its own app, nothing centralized
  • No patient tagging — can't easily go back to past leads or patients with targeted offers
  • No data sync — nothing flows automatically to a Google Sheet or CRM, no categorization, manual entry every time
  • Volume overwhelm — 100–500 DMs/week, 80% the same 5 questions (price, hours, slots, location, photos)
  • Receptionists burn out — most of the day re-typing the same answers · after-hours and weekend inquiries go cold

What we replace

  • Hiring a 2nd receptionist (~$800–1,500/mo in Egypt + the Gulf)
  • Cobbled-together Manychat / Zapier / Calendly stacks
  • Generic chatbots that hallucinate or sound robotic
  • Lost ad spend on leads that never get a reply
  • Missed appointment bookings at night & on weekends

What changes for the clinic (each pain → V1 fix)

  • Lost leads come back — every inquiry is captured and auto-tagged by source · service · status; the clinic has a clean Google Sheet of leads to follow up on (automated win-back sequences come in V2)
  • No-shows drop — automatic 24h + 2h reminders; if a patient wants to reschedule, human handoff routes to staff (multi-step rebook flow is V2)
  • One inbox for the three channels that matter — WhatsApp + Instagram + Messenger DMs unified in one dashboard (Telegram · SMS · web chat · Google Business come in V2)
  • Every patient tagged — auto-tagged by source, service, urgency, status · custom tags too · all tags flow to the Google Sheet (visual filter / retargeting dashboard is V2)
  • Auto data sync — every inquiry & booking lands in the clinic's Google Sheet with name · phone · channel · source · service · tags · status · appointment time (HubSpot / Pipedrive / Salesforce integrations are V2)
  • Bookings happen 24/7 — bot pulls free slots straight from Google Calendar, patient picks, appointment is confirmed, owner is emailed instantly
  • Owner stays in control — bot only sends the pre-approved replies the clinic wrote in the Setup Wizard · never invents text · no hallucinations

Reviews · post-visit care · satisfaction surveys · return-appointment suggestion → V2.

·The Patient Journey — Three Phases, Fully Automated

From inquiry to satisfied patient, the bot handles every step. Three phases ladder from seconds → minutes → days/weeks.

A Inquiry → Reply Seconds
  1. Patient sends a DM on WhatsApp, Instagram, or Messenger (V1 channels) — any source (ad click, story tag, website CTA, organic post, profile). More channels come in V2.
  2. Bot sends the welcome message the clinic wrote — same on every channel, every source
  3. Matches intent from the message: price · service · hours · ad-offer · book · before/after photo
  4. Sends the matching fixed reply from the clinic's reply list — predictable, on-brand
B Booking Minutes
  1. Patient signals booking intent ("I want an appointment" · "Available tomorrow?" · CTA click)
  2. Bot pulls free slots live from Google Calendar / Outlook / clinic scheduler — only inside working hours
  3. Patient picks a slot from the carousel / quick-reply buttons
  4. Bot books the appointment + syncs to CRM/Sheet + emails the owner instantly
C Lifecycle Days → weeks
  1. Reminders auto-sent 24h + 2h before the appointment to cut no-shows
  2. Reschedule / cancel — patient asks → human handoff routes to staff (auto-reschedule via bot is V2)
  3. Booking status logged in the Google Sheet — clinic owns the data and can run manual follow-ups against any tag
  4. V2 (later): 48h reminder · auto no-show recovery · post-visit care + medication · satisfaction surveys · return-appointment suggestion

The bot never writes its own messages. It just classifies, picks from the clinic's reply list, and sends. The only "live" data it inserts is the doctor's free time slots, reminder timestamps, and survey results.

Top 3 risks

1 — Channel API approval timelines. Meta WhatsApp Business + Instagram Graph + Messenger Platform API onboarding can take 2–4 weeks each (sometimes longer for new businesses). Mitigation: apply for Meta Business + WhatsApp Business in May, not July. Messenger and Instagram run on the same Meta Business approval. Have manual handoff ready as a fallback if any channel is still pending approval at launch.

2 — Clinic-owner perception + receptionist pushback. Owners may initially assume it's a typical chatbot that "talks" to patients (and worry it could say the wrong thing), or staff worry they're being replaced. Mitigation: the bot can't go off-script — it only sends replies the clinic typed itself. Demo it by showing the reply-list editor and saying "the bot can't send anything that isn't already in this list, written by you." For staff: frame it as "kills the boring 80% of repeat DMs so you focus on the patients who actually book." Every clinic gets a 60-min onboarding to write the initial reply library together.

3 — Patient data privacy (GDPR · UAE PDPL · KSA PDPL). Patient messages contain personal + sometimes health-adjacent info. Mitigation: data residency in EU or UAE where required, end-to-end encryption at rest, no sharing across clinics, clear DPA in the ToS. Avoid US HIPAA territory in Phase 1 by sticking to non-medical aesthetic clinics — we revisit for medical/dental in Phase 2.
