Project Overview

Turning leads into confirmed appointments in a unified platform for patients and doctors.

Time period
10 months
Role
Product Manager (UX-Driven)
I led the problem definition, research with doctors, system design, and coordination with business and development stakeholders.
Context
Your New Self had optimized medical lead generation, but the management of medical appointments was not yet integrated. Once the contact was assigned to a doctor, the process became fragmented between emails, external calendars, and manual follow-up. The system generated patient interest to make contact, but it did not guarantee a confirmed appointment. There was a lack of traceability, control, and an architecture connecting lead generation with real operations.
Solution
I designed and integrated a medical appointment management system within the YNS ecosystem, unifying availability, booking, and confirmation into a single platform. More than simply adding a calendar, the goal was to close the full conversion cycle within the product, giving business teams better visibility and providing doctors with operational control over their medical appointments.
02
Problem Statement

The Structural Problem of the System

Your New Self had built an efficient system to generate medical leads, but the flow broke at the real appointment booking stage — the most critical moment. Once the contact was assigned to the doctor, the process left the product and became fragmented between emails, external calendars, and manual follow-up.

The business lost visibility into which leads actually converted into confirmed appointments, and doctors operated in environments disconnected from the main system.

Research Process

Understanding Medical Operations

Understanding how doctors manage their schedules was critical to defining a viable solution.

I conducted semi-structured interviews with YNS affiliated doctors in Spain, Mexico, Italy, and Colombia to understand how they currently manage appointments, what tools they use, and the frictions they face in their daily work.

The goal was to understand how they make decisions, how they organize their availability, and what level of control they need to trust a digital system.

Operational Insights

What We Discovered

The interviews with doctors revealed clear patterns about how they manage their schedules and what conditions a digital tool must meet to be adopted in their daily practice.

Control over availability is critical

Doctors need to define flexible schedules, block specific days, and manage vacations without depending on third parties.

Rejection of complex systems

There is low tolerance for long configurations or tools with a steep learning curve.

Desktop as the main platform

Research showed that appointment management is mostly done on desktop by assistants, so the design prioritized desktop instead of mobile-first.

Fear of losing control of the schedule

Automation creates resistance if it does not provide clear visibility and the possibility of manual intervention.

Defining the Appointment System

After identifying process friction and understanding how doctors manage their schedules, the next step was to define how the system should be structured so appointment management could happen inside the platform. The goal was not just to add a calendar, but to design a flow connecting lead generation with appointment booking and management, allowing patients, doctors, and business teams to operate from a single source of information.

Design Process

Designing the Operating System

With the architecture defined, the next step was designing the interface that would allow doctors to manage appointments within the platform. The goal was to create a clear and reliable operational environment where all relevant information was visible and accessible in one place.

The system was designed as an appointment management dashboard that allows doctors to view the status of each booking, quickly access patient information, and perform actions such as confirming, modifying, or canceling appointments. The interface prioritizes clarity, information hierarchy, and quick actions to support daily schedule management.

Implementation and Results

Product Validation and Impact

The appointment management system was initially launched as a pilot in two key markets to evaluate its adoption and its impact on the booking process. This phase allowed us to measure both the improvement in appointment conversion and the operational efficiency in how doctors manage their schedules.

Operational Efficiency and Process Traceability

Centralizing appointment management restored visibility over the entire process from lead generation to confirmed appointment. As a result, more than 65% of recorded appointments were tracked within the platform, reducing the reliance on external tools.

Implementation and Scaling

The system was first launched as a pilot in Spain and Colombia to evaluate adoption and impact on the booking process. After validating the results, the feature was gradually rolled out to other markets such as Italy and Mexico, representing around 50% of YNS's global coverage.

Adoption of the System by Doctors

The system was progressively adopted by affiliated doctors, who began managing their schedules directly from the dashboard. During the first months, more than 45% of active doctors used the calendar as their main appointment management tool.

Increase in Conversion from Leads to Confirmed Appointments

Integrating the system closed the gap between lead generation and booking within the product. By offering direct confirmation of available time slots and removing manual contact, we observed an approximate +3% increase in the conversion from leads to confirmed appointments during the first months.

Product Learnings

Designing Products, Not Just Interfaces

Designing from the System, Not the Interface

This project showed that many experience problems are not solved only through the interface, but by redesigning the logic of the system. The challenge was not to create a calendar, but to close the loop between patient acquisition and real appointment management within the product.

Research as a Strategic Tool

Interviews with doctors helped us understand how they actually manage their schedules and what conditions a tool must meet to be adopted. This research was key to defining a product aligned with daily operations rather than design assumptions.

Aligning Experience, Business, and Operations

The real value of the project emerged by connecting three key parts of the product: the patient experience, the doctor's operations, and business traceability. This approach allowed us to create a viable, scalable solution aligned with the platform's goals.

Why this matters for the clinic AI receptionist plan

The product instincts behind Multiestetica — control, simplicity, desktop-first, clear status, no surprises — are the exact instincts already encoded into V1 of the clinic AI receptionist. This case study is the proof-of-execution behind that plan.
